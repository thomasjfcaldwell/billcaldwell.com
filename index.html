Cartoonist - Illustrator - Dog Owner

Born in Scotland, Bill grew up in Alexandria near Loch Lomond.He trained as a graphic designer and went into advertising in London .. got roped into doing cartoons for the odd newspaper ad and liked it. Bill's career began drawing single cartoons for the Daily Sketch, Daily Mirror and The Sun (in that order).

It all started with the atom bomb..my first drawing. I must have heard a discussion on the radio and my young ears picked up on the serious chat about the imminent destruction of the world.

It was an easy image.. I remember it clearly.Just a black filled- in pointy thing.No frills. My material got lighter with time - thank goodness- and after about 20 years it gave me a living.First in advertising then syndicating material through the Press Association to provincial newspapers in Britain . I also syndicated to the English- speaking world:Canada, Australia, New Zealand and India.An agent in the US got my work into South and Central America. This is all pre- digital age. If I ever find any thing from that time I’ll put it up here.The Daily Star launched in 1978 with me as cartoonist.Later I worked for the Sunday Mail in Scotland, the Sun and several magazines.These days I enjoy working for the Church Times and Yachting Monthly. Boats I know about. Church not so much but they show great patience with this heretic on board giving a bit of balance

I enjoy working for the Church Times. The idea is to illustrate the wordy article on the Comment page. Sort of condense grey type into something you can capture at a glance ( if you haven’t got time to wade through it all). I’ll put in only the ones that have a general appeal.
I have a boat and I sail.. This qualifies me to catalogue the mistakes and stupid things we all do when on the water. The Confessional page at the back of Yachting Monthly is where people write in to describe their yachting disasters. The best stories win a handheld radio and piss- take illustration from me. I can do this because I’ve been that idiot.
